-Three languages support boosts to manufacturing industry in Indonesia -

JM Technology Inc.(JMT) announced today that its foreign subsidiary PT. JMT INDONESIA (“JMTI”, Jakarta) has started Education Services for 3D CAD software “CATIA” in 3 languages (Japanese, English, and Indonesian), in collaboration with Fasotec. Co., Ltd. (“Fasotec”, Headquarters: Chiba, Chiba Prefecture, Japan, President: Junichi Takeuchi).

From this November, the new services will be provided for manufactures near Jakarta.

< Background of the Joint Service >

As a subsidiary of JMT, a Japanese IT company, JMTI has been carrying out the sales activities for Japanese companies in Indonesia.
CATIA education services will be provided in collaboration with Fasotec which has top class experience and know-how in sales, construction and education business of "CATIA" in Japan and China for many years. JMTI 3D CAD engineers and instructors who are fluent in three languages have been trained by JMT and Fasotec for over a year. Then JMTI succeeded to obtain Indonesian domestic education license from Dassault Systems Inc. , a seller of CATIA.

Many Japanese manufacturing industries have been entered to Indonesia with the 4th largest population in the world and largest population in Southeast Asia. Thus, Indonesia is promising as a production base for Southeast Asia and Indonesia domestic market and also expected to become a design center of products for the local market in the future.
However, compared with Thailand, a leading ASEAN development base, Indonesia development support infrastructure such as prototyping and engineering support is weak, which many manufacturers had hesitated to enter to the market.

< Future Expand >

JMTI aim to early start the services with the same quality with Japan quality for 3D CAD software “CATIA” education which is essential for the manufacturing industry and to quickly incorporate the needs into the services. From November 2015, JMTI and JMT, Fasotec aim to start the sales operations near Jakarta, a site for concentration of many Japanese manufacturers and to acquire more than 500 students until December 2016. In the future, JMTI and JMT, Fasotec plan to support the constructions and sales of the CAD system, skill diagnostic services for local 3D CAD designers, operators and to provide engineering support services.
